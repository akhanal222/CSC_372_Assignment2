Travelogue: Chitwan, Nepal

Overview

Chitwan is located in southern Nepal and is primarily known for its wildlife and wildlife conservation. It is home to 500+ species of birds and also to one-horned rhinos, Bengal tigers, elephants, and crocodiles. It also has Chitwan National Park, where visitors can see animals with jungle safaris, boat rides, and enjoy Tharu cultures.

Must-See Spots

  1. Chitwan National Park Open daily from 6 AM - 6 PM
  2. Rapti River Open daily but Best at sunset
  3. Tharu Villages Cultural tours 9 AM - 5 PM
  4. Elephant Breeding Center Open 8 AM - 6 PM
  5. Crocodile Breeding Center Open 9 AM - 4 PM
  6. Canoe Ride Morning departures recommended

Visitor Tips

  • The best time to visit Chitwan is between October and March because in this season the grass will be cut down and you will be able to see wildlife easily.
  • If you planning to do Jeep safari you need to wear light colored clothes so animal will not notice you.
  • Carry binoculars if you are planning to bird watching or Safari
  • Book for safari and canoe rides as it booked faster in peak season between October and March.
